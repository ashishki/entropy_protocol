# Entropy Protocol — Research Firewall

**Version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** 2026-03-16
**Purpose:** Define the boundary between research discovery work and the main evaluation protocol.

---

## Policy

No AI-generated research object may enter the protocol evaluation pipeline without explicit human registration in the Trial Registry.

This rule applies to:
- AI-generated hypotheses
- AI-suggested feature definitions
- AI-generated regime tags
- AI-generated signal modifications
- AI-generated experiment proposals derived from the Research Discovery Layer (RDL)

Registration must occur before any evaluation-window data is examined.

---

## Boundary

### Research Discovery Layer

The Research Discovery Layer is a discovery environment.

Its role is to:
- generate candidate hypotheses
- structure candidate objects
- define candidate features
- label candidate events or regimes for research preparation

The RDL does not grant evaluation rights by itself.

### Main Evaluation Protocol

The Main Evaluation Protocol is the governed path that produces admissible evidence.

Its role is to:
- accept only pre-registered trials
- enforce walk-forward separation
- count multiplicity through the Trial Registry
- produce evaluation records that can affect phase gates, kill logic, and promotion decisions

---

## Admissibility Rule

A research object becomes admissible only when all of the following are true:
- a human sponsor has reviewed it
- a Trial Registry entry has been created explicitly
- the object has been assigned to a hypothesis family
- the experiment has passed the Experiment Readiness Gate

Until then, the object remains research-only and cannot:
- enter the walk-forward harness as a valid trial
- contribute to phase-gate evidence
- influence portfolio routing, sizing, or risk escalation
- be cited as OOS evidence

---

## Why This Exists

The firewall prevents:
- hidden trial inflation through informal AI ideation
- post-hoc signal shaping after data exposure
- accidental collapse of the boundary between discovery and evaluation
- governance drift between RDL activity and the main protocol

This control preserves the protocol rule that evidence must be pre-registered, auditable, and multiplicity-aware.
