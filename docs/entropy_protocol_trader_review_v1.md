## A. What This Framework Is (and Is Not)

### What It Is

This is a **Capital Allocation Framework** (CAF), not a trading diary and not a discretionary swing-trading system.

What that means in practice:
- Decisions are generated by systematic rules, not real-time judgment
- Every entry/exit is logged, timestamped, and attributable to a pre-specified signal
- Performance is evaluated against a pre-defined baseline, not against "how it felt"
- A losing month only triggers action if it crosses a pre-specified statistical threshold

The system's realistic mature form: a systematic portfolio that generates moderate trading alpha (net Sharpe ~0.28–0.42 in the base case) and allocates idle capital to yield (T-bills or Tier-1 staking). These two sources are always reported separately — the trading alpha must stand on its own.

The classification "CAF" is conditional. The system starts as a **Research Lab**. It only becomes a CAF if Phase 1 exit criteria are met after ≥15 months of out-of-sample evaluation.

### What It Is Not

- **Not a discretionary swing diary.** External ideas do not go directly into position sizing. They enter as pre-registered hypotheses and are tested systematically.
- **Not a signal aggregator.** The framework doesn't blend your view with a quant signal and call it "combined edge." Your input enters via a defined process (see Section E) or not at all.
- **Not a market-making or high-frequency system.** Timeframes: 4-hour bars and daily bars. Holding periods: typically 3–15 days.
- **Not leveraged.** Gross exposure = |longs| + |shorts| ≤ 100% of capital at all times.

### Why That Matters for Your Contribution

Your value in this framework is not as a discretionary decision-maker. Your value is as a **hypothesis generator**: someone who can identify patterns, reference levels, or regime signals from onchain/macro observation that can be converted into pre-registered, rule-based hypotheses and tested properly.

## B. How the Strategy Makes Decisions

The system has three layers, each with its own function:

### Layer 1: Skills (Signal Generation)

5–6 base strategy types run on 4H and 1D data across ~20 liquid assets (top-4 per sector: large-cap equities, crypto majors, commodities, FX):

| Skill cluster | What it does |
|---|---|
| Trend / Momentum | Detects and bets on persistent price direction |
| Reversion | Bets against overextended moves in range-bound conditions |
| Volatility regime | Filters entries based on realized volatility environment |

Skills generate raw signal scores. They do not determine position sizes.

### Layer 2: Portfolio Construction

Takes skill signals and determines actual position sizes based on:
- **Volatility targeting:** scales down when realized vol is elevated (5-day lag max)
- **Correlation control:** if 20-day average pairwise correlation across the portfolio exceeds 0.55, gross exposure is cut 35–50% over 3 days. This is what prevents 20% drawdown from becoming 40%.
- **Regime routing:** adjusts how signals are weighted based on the current market regime (trending vs. mean-reverting vs. stress)

### Layer 3: Regime Signals (Risk Management)

Four regime-adjacent signals, in strict priority order:

| Priority | Signal | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (highest) | Drawdown circuit breaker | Drawdown from peak ≥ 12% | Cut all positions to 50%; pause 5 days |
| 2 | Crypto funding exit | Perp funding > 0.05%/8hr × 3 consecutive windows | Exit all crypto shorts |
| 3 | Correlation spike | 20-day avg pairwise ρ > 0.55 | Cut gross 35–50% over 3 days |
| 4 | Weekly regime overlay | 1W regime state | Adjust routing within current exposure |

Higher priority always overrides lower. These are hard rules — they are not optimization knobs.

### The Sequence

The system starts **long-only** on 4H/1D data. Short positions and the weekly regime overlay are added only after the long-only baseline meets exit criteria (≥15 months of out-of-sample data). This takes time. There is no shortcut.

## C. Where Your Onchain / Macro Ideas Can Plug In

### The Short Answer

Your ideas enter as **hypotheses only** — not live portfolio signals. The process converts a narrative observation into a rule, pre-registers it, tests it systematically, and determines whether the evidence supports promotion to the main system.

There are two categories of hypotheses you can inform:

### Category 1: Regime Risk Flags (Macro / Structural)

These are signals that might affect *how much risk* the system takes, not which direction to trade.

Examples that fit the framework:
- "Crypto perp funding has been > 0.10%/8hr for 5 consecutive days" → does that predict a higher probability of a sharp correction in the next 7 days?
- "Realized correlations across crypto majors have been rising for 3 weeks" → does that predict the P3 correlation trigger firing within 10 days?
- "Exchange net flow (large outflows over 14 days)" → does that have predictive power for crypto price direction at 4H–1D timeframe?

For these to enter testing: the rule must be written precisely (numeric threshold, lookback, direction) before any recent chart is examined.

### Category 2: Exit Overlays (Reference Levels)

These are signals that affect *when* an existing position exits, not whether to enter.

Examples that fit the framework:
- "Prior weekly high/low" → does reaching a prior weekly H/L in a trending trade systematically improve final P&L vs holding until a time-based exit?
- "VWAP at session open" → does price reaching VWAP represent a reference level that improves exits for reversion trades?
- "ATR-multiple targets (1.5×, 2×)": do vol-adjusted targets improve per-trade expectancy?

These are the first batch being tested in the Acceleration Track (2–3 months of paper trading, pre-registered, no capital at risk).

### Category 3: Stress Triggers / Tail Risk Early Warnings

These are signals that might trigger early de-risking before the model's correlation or drawdown triggers fire.

Examples:
- "Unusual options skew across top-5 crypto assets" → precedes correlation spike within 5 days?
- "Stablecoin depegs > X bps" → associated with crypto contagion within N days?
- "Large miner selling pressure on-chain" → associated with BTC drawdown within 2 weeks?

For these to enter testing: the causal chain must be written down. What is the mechanism? What is the expected lead time? What is the base rate historically?

### What You Cannot Do

- You cannot submit a chart and say "this looks like a good setup" — there must be a precise rule
- You cannot view recent data first and then write the rule — the rule must be written on data older than 60 days relative to submission
- Your hypothesis cannot change the entry signal of the main portfolio during testing — exit overlays and regime flags only

## D. Why We Separate: Four P&L Streams

The portfolio generates returns from four distinct sources. These are **always reported separately**:

| Stream | What it is |
|---|---|
| (a) Long P&L | Returns from long positions driven by skills |
| (b) Short P&L | Returns from short positions (Phase 3+ only) |
| (c) Borrow / Funding P&L | Cost of maintaining shorts: equity borrow + crypto perp funding rate |
| (d) Treasury P&L | Yield on idle capital: T-bills or Tier-1 staking |

The **primary performance metric** (net Sharpe) is computed on streams (a)+(b)+(c) only. Stream (d) is a secondary figure, always labeled separately.

**Why this matters:** If the trading system (a+b+c) is barely breaking even and treasury yield (d) makes the total look fine, that is a failure — the trading complexity is unjustified. This separation forces honest accounting. A kill criterion (K5) fires if stream (d) exceeds 60% of total return in any 12-month period, triggering a strategic review.

**For crypto shorts specifically (stream c):** Perpetual funding costs are **not a tail risk**. In sustained bull markets, funding rates of 0.05%–0.15%/8hr (55–165% annualized) are routine. A 30% short book with 50% in crypto perps at 15% avg annualized funding = 2.25% NAV drag per year. The expected gross benefit from the short side at realistic IC assumptions is ~0.55% additional return. The net expected value is negative in this scenario. This is why the base plan for Phase 4 (crypto perp shorts) is to bypass it.

## E. The Acceleration Track: Testing Ideas Fast Without Fooling Ourselves

### What It Is

The Acceleration Track (AT) is a 2–3 month paper-trading process for hypotheses that are too scoped to require a full 15-month OOS evaluation. It runs alongside Phase 0 (infrastructure build) and is paper-only.

### How It Works

1. **Pre-register the hypothesis.** Before any recent data is examined, write a one-page spec:
   - What is the rule exactly?
   - What is the entry condition? (locked — cannot change)
   - What are we testing? (the exit, the overlay, the filter)
   - What is the baseline we compare against? (also paper-traded)
   - What metric do we compute?
   - What threshold classifies as "promising"?
   - How many trades are needed before we evaluate?

2. **Paper-trade for 2–3 months.** Both the hypothesis and the baseline run simultaneously. No capital at risk.

3. **Evaluate.** If the pre-registered threshold is met, the hypothesis is labeled "promising" — not "validated." It enters the queue for Phase 1 evaluation.

### What 2–3 Months CAN Tell Us

- Which exit overlay variants are worth advancing (plausibility screen)
- Whether a specific stop fires so frequently it is disqualified by turnover alone
- Whether a reference level has appeared enough times historically to be worth testing
- Whether an influencer-derived setup has a base rate sufficient for systematic study

### What 2–3 Months CANNOT Tell Us

- Whether the system has positive net Sharpe
- Whether the portfolio is viable
- Whether the framework can survive two different market regimes
- Whether to deploy capital

A promising AT result is not permission to deploy capital. It is permission to put the idea in the Phase 1 queue.

### Why This Isn't Just Backtesting

Backtesting on past data is implicit trial-and-error with the results visible. The AT uses:
- **Pre-registration:** the hypothesis is written before examining the evaluation data
- **Paper execution:** signals are generated in real-time, not retroactively
- **Parallel baseline:** the control always runs alongside the hypothesis
- **Separation:** AT results are never labeled "OOS"; they cannot be combined with walk-forward results

## F. What Kinds of Ideas Are Welcome

**High-value idea types:**
- Observable, rule-based regime indicators (funding rates, exchange flows, options skew, breadth metrics)
- Reference levels that many market participants visibly use (prior week H/L, monthly H/L, VWAP)
- Tail risk early warnings with a plausible mechanism and historical base rate
- Regime context for existing signals (e.g., "trend signals underperform in high-funding environments — here's the rule")
- Qualitative context about which markets are structurally broken or unusual (informs universe review)

**Lower-value idea types (harder to test):**
- Discretionary chart patterns without a precise rule
- Setups derived from recent market action (< 60 days ago) without historical precedent
- Ideas that require looking at specific recent trades first
- Ideas about specific assets that are not in the top-4 of their sector

**Ideas that cannot enter the AT:**
- Rules that change the entry signal of the main portfolio (those require a Phase 1 test)
- Rules that involve data that cannot be obtained historically (no base rate check possible)
- Ideas expressed as narratives rather than precise, computable conditions

## G. What Data / Evidence Would Convince Us

For an idea to be promoted from AT to Phase 1 evaluation:

| Standard | What is required |
|---|---|
| Pre-specified metric | Meets the pre-registered threshold (not just "looks good") |
| Sample size | Minimum trades reached before evaluation |
| Baseline comparison | The control ran in parallel; the hypothesis exceeded the baseline on the pre-specified metric |
| Harvey-Liu accounting | The hypothesis trial count is logged; multiplicity correction applied |
| Phase 1 registration | A matching Phase 1 test is registered before any Phase 1 data for that dimension is examined |

For a regime flag hypothesis to pass screening:
- Historical base rate computed on ≥3 years of data: does the setup occur ≥2× per month on average?
- Proposed causal mechanism written down before examining outcomes
- Lag between signal and event estimated from historical data, not assumed

For a quantitative claim to be taken seriously (beyond the AT):
- More than 40 matched pairs or 100 trades (rough minimums)
- Point estimate with confidence interval, not just direction
- Comparison to a stated baseline, not to zero

## H. Questions for You (Trader Question List)

These questions are meant to collect structured input. Each should ideally produce a specific, falsifiable answer rather than a general opinion.

### Signal Ideas — Precise Definitions

1. **Funding rate as regime signal:** At what funding rate threshold (e.g., 0.04%/8hr? 0.05%? 0.08%?) do you observe that long traders begin visibly closing positions, and what is the typical time lag between the rate hitting that threshold and the price reaction you are describing? Can you point to 5+ historical examples from the last 3 years?

2. **Exchange flow signal:** What specific on-chain metric (net exchange inflow/outflow in BTC/ETH, large-wallet accumulation index, CEX reserve change?) do you use as a leading indicator, and over what lookback period? Have you counted how many times this signal occurred in the 2020–2025 period without a subsequent price reaction?

3. **Reference levels:** Which price levels do you observe the crypto community treating as meaningful reference points most consistently — prior weekly H/L, prior monthly H/L, a specific MA (50D, 200D), VWAP? Can you give a rule for how you identify when a level is "in play" vs. ignored?

4. **Volatility regime:** What observable condition do you associate with "the market being in a trending vs. choppy state" in crypto specifically? Is it a ratio of realized vol to implied vol, a breadth indicator, or something else? What is the lookback period you use?

5. **Altcoin relative performance:** Do you observe a systematic relationship between BTC dominance direction and altcoin performance at 4H–1D timeframe? What is the rule, and what is the expected lead time?

### Regime / Tail Risk Perspectives

6. **Contagion early warning:** What is the first observable signal that you use to anticipate a liquidity crisis or forced-liquidation cascade in crypto? Exchange stablecoin reserve changes? Options open interest cliff? Something else? How many days of advance warning have you observed historically?

7. **Macro correlation regimes:** In your observation, when does crypto-equity correlation become a problem for a diversified portfolio (i.e., when do they move together rather than independently)? Is this primarily driven by a DXY threshold, a VIX level, or something specific to crypto market structure?

8. **Crypto bear market structure:** In the 2022 bear market and the 2021 corrections, what signals — if any — were available on-chain or in perp structure that preceded the major drawdowns by more than a week? Were any of these signals systematic (computable by rule) rather than retrospectively obvious?

9. **Regime mismatch:** Are there specific market conditions where you believe a systematic trend-following approach on 4H–1D bars is structurally likely to fail? What would you look for as a leading indicator of that regime transition?

### Influencer Bias and Base Rates

10. **Influencer setup frequency:** For the most common setup types promoted by crypto influencers you follow (e.g., "ascending triangle breakout," "support retest," "weekly close above X"), how often do you estimate these setups occur per asset per month historically? Have you ever counted?

11. **Narrative vs. price action:** In your experience, when a narrative is widely circulated in crypto (e.g., "ETH/BTC bottomed"), what is the base rate at which the price action actually follows the narrative within 30 days vs. reverting or doing something unrelated?

12. **Source quality:** Do you know of any crypto analysts or onchain data providers where you have tracked their directional calls over ≥12 months and computed the hit rate? What was the rate, and on what timeframe were they calling?

### Markets and Periods That Break Assumptions

13. **2021 bull market:** The system's correlation trigger fires at 20-day ρ > 0.55. In 2021, was the realized correlation among crypto majors above 0.55 for extended periods even during the bull market? If yes, this means the system would have been systematically deleveraged during a trending regime — is that the right behavior?

14. **USD stablecoin environment:** If USD stablecoins experience a systemic stress event, the portfolio's ability to rebalance, pay out, or exit positions becomes impaired. What is your assessment of the current risk profile of major stablecoins (USDT, USDC) and how often do you expect a depeg event of >10bps to occur?

15. **Crypto market structure changes:** The framework was designed on 4H–1D bars of liquid assets. Are there structural changes in crypto market microstructure since 2023 (ETF flows, institutional market-making, derivatives dominance) that you think invalidate assumptions the system makes about signal persistence or cost of carry?

### Practical Execution Gotchas

16. **Perp funding in practice:** At what funding rate level do you personally consider a short position in crypto perps no longer worth holding, and how do you handle the decision to exit vs. wait? Is your threshold higher or lower than 0.05%/8hr?

17. **Slippage in crypto during stress:** At what order size (in USD notional, relative to 24h volume) does slippage become material for top-5 crypto assets on spot exchanges vs perp exchanges? The cost model uses 0.02%/fill market impact — is that realistic for a $50k–$200k notional position in stress conditions?

18. **On-chain data latency:** For the on-chain metrics you use (exchange flows, whale tracking, miner data), what is the realistic data lag from on-chain event to reliable API availability? Is same-day data consistently reliable, or are there systematic reporting lags?

19. **Exchange risk concentration:** Given the FTX collapse, what is your current practice for limiting exchange concentration risk? At what single-exchange notional exposure would you start distributing across multiple venues?

20. **Liquidity tiers:** Among the top-20 crypto assets by market cap, which ones have 4H bid-ask spreads that would make 0.05%/leg cost assumptions unreliable? Are there any assets in the top-20 where a 4% position (vs. 5% target) would be difficult to exit cleanly within 2 trading sessions?
