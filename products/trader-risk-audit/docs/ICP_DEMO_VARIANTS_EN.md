# ICP Demo Variants EN

Use these variants to adapt the founder-led demo. Do not split product
implementation by ICP until real outreach evidence shows which group converts.
Every variant is the same product: post-trade audit, no broker control, no
signal analytics, not investment advice, does not control live trading, and no
order blocking.

Every variant maps to the same validation evidence gate, not vanity metrics:
10 qualified prospects, 5 exports/rules, 3 paid audits, and 2 repeat audit
commitments.

## Prop/Funded Traders

- ICP: prop/funded traders.
- Likely pain: payout risk, max daily loss, drawdown, cooldown, and forbidden
  behavior rules can end an account even when the trader thinks the plan was
  mostly followed.
- Current workaround: platform dashboard, spreadsheet, journal screenshots, or
  coach review after the fact.
- Demo angle: show source row ids, repeated rule breaches, affected P&L, and
  limitations from a deterministic report instead of a subjective recap.
- Required proof: real trade export, written prop/funded rules, and operator
  approval for any ambiguous rule mapping.
- Paid pilot ask: buy one manual audit for the most recent export period and
  decide whether a repeat audit is useful before the next payout window.

## Active Crypto Discretionary Traders

- ICP: active crypto discretionary traders.
- Likely pain: fast sessions, multiple symbols, revenge trades, oversized
  positions, and weak cooldown discipline are hard to reconstruct from exchange
  exports alone.
- Current workaround: exchange CSV, manual notes, screenshots, TradingView
  annotations, or a private journal.
- Demo angle: compare raw export rows with deterministic rule checks, source row
  ids, violation-attributed P&L, and a concise next-review checklist.
- Required proof: real trade export, written risk rules, session timezone, and
  account currency without API keys or wallet/broker credentials.
- Paid pilot ask: pay for one manual audit and review whether the report finds
  discipline breaks faster than the current journal.

## Small Teams/Coaches

- ICP: small teams/coaches.
- Likely pain: a coach or team lead needs consistent review across traders
  without reading every private note or inventing rule interpretations.
- Current workaround: shared spreadsheets, screenshots, Notion pages, group
  calls, or ad hoc manual review.
- Demo angle: show how the same policy template can produce per-trader reports
  with source row ids, rule breaches, limitations, and repeatable evidence.
- Required proof: one anonymized team/trader export, written team rules, privacy
  agreement, and explicit operator approval for policy mapping.
- Paid pilot ask: pay for a small manual audit pack for one to three traders and
  decide whether repeat audits or referrals are worth scheduling.

## Boundary

These are positioning variants only. They do not create separate products,
dashboards, broker integrations, signal analytics, or advice flows. Use the
same validation evidence gate and evidence log for all variants.
