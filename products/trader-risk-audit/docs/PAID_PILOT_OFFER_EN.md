# Paid Pilot Offer EN

Trader Risk Audit is a founder-led manual audit for traders who already have
executed trades and written risk rules. This is a static paid pilot offer, not
a SaaS checkout, account system, broker connection, or public landing-page app.

## Deliverables

- One manual audit report for one export period.
- Deterministic rule breach table with source row ids.
- Violation-attributed P&L impact.
- Limitations for missing or unsupported fields.
- Telegram-ready summary copy after operator review.
- Short review call or async review notes.

## Required Inputs

- Real trade export for the audit period.
- Written risk rules or prop/funded account rules.
- Timezone, session window, account currency, and audit period.
- Confirmation that the files do not contain API keys, passwords, seed phrases,
  broker tokens, payment identifiers, or unnecessary personal data.

## Timeline

- Intake review: same day when files are complete.
- Manual audit: 48-72 hours after complete inputs and operator-approved mapping.
- Delivery: report and summary after claim-boundary review.

## Privacy Boundary

Files stay local by default. The pilot intake contract defines allowed files and
metadata. Do not send real names, Telegram handles, emails, broker account ids,
screenshots with personal data, payment identifiers, API keys, passwords, or
seed phrases.

## No-Advice Boundary

This audit is not investment advice and does not control live trading. It has
no broker control, no broker APIs, no signal parsing, no order blocking, no
live risk prevention, no performance prediction, and no guaranteed improvement.
It is not PMF evidence by itself; PMF and market validation require paid pilot
evidence.

## Pilot Price Placeholder

Pilot price placeholder: `$49-$149` for one manual audit report. Final price is
confirmed manually before work starts. No checkout flow is implemented here.

## Paid Pilot CTA

Send a real trade export and written risk rules, approve any mapping questions,
and buy one paid pilot manual audit. The goal is to test whether the report is
useful enough to repeat, not to request more features.
