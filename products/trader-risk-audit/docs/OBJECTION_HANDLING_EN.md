# Objection Handling EN

Use this during founder-led paid pilot calls. It is sales enablement, not legal
advice, not investment advice, and not compliance advice.

## Privacy

Answer: "The pilot intake contract asks for local files only: trade export,
written risk rules, and non-sensitive intake metadata. Do not send real names,
Telegram handles, emails, broker account ids, screenshots with personal data,
payment identifiers, API keys, passwords, seed phrases, or raw private notes."

## Broker API

Objection: "Can you connect to my broker?"

Answer: "The current stable pilot uses exported files. The next planned roadmap
may add read-only Binance/Bybit import for historical fills, but that is not
broker control: no trading permission, no withdrawals, no transfers, no order
blocking, and no live account access."

## No Advice

Objection: "Will you tell me what to trade?"

Answer: "No advice. The audit checks executed trades against written rules. It
does not tell you what to buy or sell, does not control live trading, and makes
no performance promise."

## Why Not My Journal

Objection: "Why not my journal?"

Answer: "A journal can be useful, but it often leaves rule checks manual. This
report adds deterministic rule checks, source row ids, violation-attributed
P&L, limitations, and a next-review checklist."

## Pricing

Objection: "Why pay for this?"

Answer: "The paid pilot is a manual audit report. You are paying to test whether
the report is useful enough to repeat, not buying a SaaS account or live risk
control."

## Repeat Audit

Objection: "Why repeat it?"

Answer: "A repeat audit shows whether the same rule breaches keep appearing and
whether the review process is worth using again. It still has no signal parsing,
no live control, and no performance guarantee."

## Gate References

Point back to the pilot intake contract and paid pilot evidence gate:

- 10 qualified prospects;
- 5 exports/rules;
- 3 paid audits;
- 2 repeat audit commitments.

Public sample/demo artifacts do not count toward this gate.
